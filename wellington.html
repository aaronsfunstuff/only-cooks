Beef Wellington

Prep Time: 1 hour

Cook Time: 45 minutes

Difficulty: Hard

Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 1.2 kg beef fillet (center-cut piece)
  • 500g mixture of wild mushrooms, cleaned
  • 1 shallot, peeled and chopped
  • 2 tablespoons English mustard
  • 12 slices of Parma ham
  • 500g puff pastry
  • 2 egg yolks, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F).
  2. Season the beef fillet with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a pan and sear the beef on all sides until browned. Remove from pan and allow to cool. Brush all over with mustard.
  3. In a food processor, blend the mushrooms and shallot into a fine paste. Cook this mixture in a dry pan over medium heat until all the moisture has evaporated, stirring often.
  4. Lay out a sheet of cling film on a work surface and arrange the Parma ham slices, overlapping them slightly. Spread the mushroom paste over the ham.
  5. Place the beef fillet on the mushroom-covered ham and roll it up tightly, using the cling film to help. Twist the ends to seal and refrigerate for 15 minutes.
  6. Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface. Remove the cling film from the beef and place it on the pastry. Wrap the pastry around the beef and seal the edges.
  7. Brush the pastry with beaten egg yolks. Chill the wrapped beef for another 15 minutes to let it set.
  8. Place the Beef Wellington on a baking tray and bake for 35-40 minutes until the pastry is golden brown. Rest before slicing.

Nutrition Facts

Nutrient Amount (per serving)
Calories 890 kcal
Protein 42 g
Fat 55 g
Carbohydrates 54 g
Fiber 4 g

Tips

For an even richer flavor, wrap the beef in a layer of crepes before encasing it in pastry. This technique helps seal in the juices and creates an additional layer of taste.

Recipe by Gordon Ramsay
